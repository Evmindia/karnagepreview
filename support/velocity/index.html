Quick Setup Guide

Sr. no Question Answer
1 How do I connect Velocity Headsets? Unbox the headset and remove any protective films. Adjust the headband and earcups for a secure, comfortable fit. Connect the attached cable to your host device.
2 How do I power it ON/OFF? Wired headsets do not require a power button — they become active as soon as they are plugged into the host device.
3 How do I adjust the Mic? The microphone is Omnidirectional; simply position it near your mouth for optimal clarity.
4 How do I adjust volume? Use the inline volume controller on the Left Earcup for immediate level changes. Also confirm system and application volume on the host.

Features and Customization

Sr. no Question Answer
1 What is the driver size in the headset? 50mm drivers optimized with virtual 7.1 processing to reproduce positional, multi-channel audio.
2 What is 7.1 virtual surround? Software-driven multi-channel simulation that provides positional audio for gaming—enabled when using the USB interface and PC software.
3 What kind of microphone is used? Omnidirectional microphone with noise-reduction tuning for clear in-game voice.
4 Does it have swappable earpads? Yes, user-replaceable earpads for quick removal and replacement (for hygiene or customization).
5 Are there inline controls? Yes, volume controls are located on the Left Earcup.
6 Is dedicated software available? Yes — a dedicated PC application provides EQ presets, virtual 7.1 toggle, mic gain settings, and voice effects.

Power Management

Sr. no Question Answer
1 What is the power input requirement? The headset is USB-powered and does not contain an internal battery.
2 Does it have auto shut-off? No, as the headset is USB-powered and active whenever plugged in.

Compatibility

Sr. no Question Answer
1 Which platforms are supported? Windows, macOS, iOS, and Android systems.
2 Can it be used with gaming consoles or TVs? Yes, basic stereo audio is supported on consoles/TVs, but virtual 7.1 and PC software features are not available.
3 Does it support mobile devices? Yes, basic stereo audio is supported on mobile devices.

Troubleshooting

Sr. no Question Answer
1 Why is there no audio?
  • Confirm the headset is firmly connected.
  • Check system playback device: set "Karnage Velocity" (or Hyperion) as default.
  • Increase system and application volume.
  • Try a different USB port to isolate the issue.
2 Why is the microphone not working? Set the Velocity microphone as the default input device in OS settings and check for app-specific permissions.
3 Why aren’t the inline controls responding? Reconnect the headset directly to the host (avoid hubs) and confirm the OS recognizes the device.
4 Why is audio only in one ear? Check stereo balance in OS sound settings and inspect the cable/connector for damage.
